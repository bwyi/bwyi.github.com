Systèmes multivariables - Multivariable Systems

Language of instruction for Fall 2026: English.

Course Description

This is a graduate-level course on the theory of linear multivariable dynamical systems. It introduces mathematical modelling, state-space and input–output representations, stability, controllability, observability, and realization theory. The course also covers the design of state-feedback controllers and observers. Familiarity with linear algebra, calculus, and basic control theory is recommended.

Textbooks and Reference Materials

There is no required textbook. Reading materials include:

  • W. J. Rugh, Linear System Theory, 1996. ★
  • F. M. Callier and C. A. Desoer, Linear System Theory. ★
  • T. Kailath, Linear Systems, Prentice-Hall, 1980.
  • P. J. Antsaklis and A. N. Michel, Linear Systems, 1997. (Online textbook available to students at Polytechnique)
  • Lecture notes for the course 6.241J (Dynamic Systems and Control) at MIT, by M. Dahleh, M. Dahleh, and G. Verghese. (Available online)
  • J. P. Hespanha, Linear Systems Theory, 2018. (Textbook sold at the COOP)
  • Chi-Tsong Chen, Linear System Theory and Design, 3rd ed., 1999. (Available online)

Grading Policy

Homework: 6 graded homework assignments (5% each; 30% total)

Homework must be completed and submitted individually. Sharing final answers or detailed solution methods is not permitted. Violations will be reported to Polytechnique and handled under the academic integrity policy.

Exams:

  • Midterm exam (30%, October 7)
  • Final exam (40%, date TBA)
Open-book exams: Both the midterm and final exams are open-book. You may bring any printed or handwritten paper materials. Computers and other electronic devices are not permitted.

Late submissions:

  • 15% penalty if submitted less than 12 hours late
  • 30% penalty if submitted between 12 and 24 hours late
  • A grade of 0 if submitted more than 24 hours late
Generative AI tools are permitted, subject to Polytechnique’s Utilisation des outils d’intelligence artificielle générative policy.
