I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in Robotics Institute at the University of Technology Sydney in the research group led by Dikai Liu. Prior to this, I was a Postdoctoral Research Associate in Australian Centre for Field Robotics (ACFR) at the University of Sydney, hosted by Ian Manchester working on the ARC Discovery Project ``Robust learning of dynamic systems'' from September 2019 to September 2022. I obtained my Ph.D. degree in Control Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University, China in 2019 under the supervision of Weidong Zhang. During which, I was a visiting student at Laboratoire des Signaux et Systèmes (L2S), CNRS-CentraleSupélec, France from October 2017 to April 2019, as a member of Romeo Ortega's group.

My research interests include nonlinear and adaptive control, observer design, system identification, with special emphasis on applications to robotics. Dr. Yi received the IEEE CCTA Best Student Paper Award from IEEE Control Systems Society for his work on sensorless observer design for IPMSMs in 2019.
