I am currently a postdoc research associate of Australian Centre for Field Robotics (ACFR) at the University of Sydney. I obtained my Ph.D. degree in Systems and Control from Shanghai Jiao Tong University, China in 2019. Before that, I was a visiting student at Laboratoire des Signaux et Systèmes, CNRS-CentraleSupélec, France from Oct 2017 to Apr 2019.

My research interests include nonlinear and adaptive control, observer design, system identification, with special emphasis on applications to robotics.

Dr. Yi received the IEEE Conference on Control Technology and Applications (CCTA) Best Student Paper Award from IEEE Control Systems Society for his work on sensorless observer design for IPMSMs in 2019.

 

Recent News

  • 11 Dec 2020 We propose a novel method to design reduced-order observers for nonlinear systems via contraction analysis and convex optimization. Our paper, collobrating with Ruigang Wang and Ian R. Manchester, has been submitted. Its preprint may be found at arXiv:2007.15224.
  • 20 Oct 2020 Our paper, in which we propose the first provable globally convergent sensorless observer for IPMSMs, was accepted by Automatica.

  • 27 Sept 2020 We submitted a paper to American Control Conference with Ruigang Wang and Ian R. Manchester. [Proposing a novel reduced-order constructive method via contraction analysis and convex optimisation]
