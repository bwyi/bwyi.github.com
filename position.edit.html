Research opportunities · Montréal

Open Positions

I am interested in working with motivated students and researchers in control, estimation, learning, and robotics. The group values strong theoretical foundations, clear thinking, intellectual independence, and a genuine interest in meaningful engineering problems.

General inquiries welcome

Prospective graduate students, postdoctoral researchers, and research visitors are welcome to get in touch. Specific funded openings depend on current projects and available funding.

Last updated: July 2026

Research Directions

Nonlinear & Adaptive Control Stability, observer-based control, parameter estimation, and learning in dynamical systems.
Data-Driven Control & Estimation System identification, input–output methods, and reliable learning from experimental data.
Autonomous & Multi-Robot Systems Coordination, source seeking, distributed algorithms, and control of robot swarms.
Navigation, SLAM & State Estimation Geometric estimation, sensor fusion, visual–inertial navigation, and mobile robotics.

Opportunities

Graduate research

Ph.D. and Research-Based M.Sc. Students

I regularly consider strong applicants for research-based graduate studies at Polytechnique Montréal. Suitable backgrounds include control systems, applied mathematics, electrical or mechanical engineering, robotics, and related fields.

Applicants should be comfortable with linear algebra and calculus. Experience with dynamical systems, probability, optimization, programming, or robotics is an asset.

Funding dependent

Postdoctoral Researchers

Postdoctoral positions are occasionally available, subject to project funding. Strong candidates are encouraged to contact me early to discuss research fit and external fellowship applications.

Competitive postdoctoral funding programs in Canada and Québec include:

  • Banting Postdoctoral FellowshipsLegacy program; applications have transitioned to the CPRA.
  • Canada Postdoctoral Research AwardCurrent federal program, formerly the NSERC PDF.
  • Mitacs Accelerate for PostdocsCurrent model, formerly Mitacs Elevate.
  • FRQ Postdoctoral ScholarshipsNature and technologies sector, formerly FRQNT B3.
  • IVADO Postdoctoral Research FundingFor research aligned with robust, reasoning, and responsible AI.
  • GERAD Postdoctoral FellowshipCompetitive co-supervised postdoctoral funding.
Research visits

Visiting Students and Research Interns

I welcome visiting graduate students, undergraduate research interns, and academic visitors for short- or long-term stays. Visitors are normally expected to have external funding from their home institution or a scholarship program.

How to Apply

Start with a concise email

Use the subject line “Prospective PhD/MSc/Postdoc – Name – Intended start term” and briefly explain why your background and interests fit the group.

Email to Apply
  • A focused description of your research interests and intended degree or position.
  • A short research-fit statement explaining how your background connects to one of the directions above.
  • Your CV and academic transcripts; unofficial transcripts are sufficient for the initial inquiry.
  • Links to publications, preprints, code, a thesis, or relevant projects, if available.
  • Your preferred start term and any fellowship or external funding plans.
Formal admission is handled centrally by Polytechnique Montréal. Candidates must meet the university's graduate admission requirements. Research-based M.Sc. and Ph.D. programs may be completed in English, while course and language requirements depend on the selected program.
